Objective of Report

The Turkey Lipton Scorecard is built to track overall Ice Tea market performance across every format sold in Turkey, and to benchmark Lipton's own products against named competitors on a consistent, repeatable basis every period.

Business Question

"How is the Ice Tea category and our brand performing across Turkish markets and formats this period, and where are we gaining or losing ground against competitors?"

Primary Use Case

  • Recurring brand scorecard - standardised monthly/quarterly/YTD view of category and brand performance
  • Competitive benchmarking - direct comparison of Lipton products against competitor products at flavour and format level
  • Portfolio contribution tracking - identifies which flavours/formats are driving growth vs. dragging on it

Interim Data Cuts

Category-Level Cut

Total Ice Tea category performance across all of Turkey's tracked market channels - the denominator for every share calculation in the scorecard.

  • Total category value/volume/unit sales by market channel, trended L3M / YTD / YTD YA
  • Category share movement and growth rate across the nine Turkish market definitions
  • Category-level distribution and ROS benchmarks

Brand / Flavour-Level Cut

The primary stakeholder-requested cut - Lipton's performance broken out by flavour and format, benchmarked against named competitors.

  • Brand and flavour-level value/volume share vs. competitive set
  • % of Portfolio - calculated contribution of each flavour/format/pack type to total brand sales
  • Contribution to Growth - calculated share of total category or brand growth attributable to each flavour
  • Format and pack-type performance within each flavour

Channel-Level Cut

  • Discounter performance (BIM & A101 & SOK) vs. Total Downline and Supermarket channels
  • East Trad vs. West Trad regional comparison
  • NKA/LKA (National/Local Key Account) channel comparison
06

Insights Generation

Insight Focus Areas

  • Share movement - which flavours/formats are gaining or losing share within the category
  • Growth contribution - which portfolio elements are driving or dragging on overall brand growth
  • Channel dynamics - where performance differs materially across Turkey's nine market/channel definitions
  • Distribution & ROS - whether sales velocity and distribution trends support or undermine the share story

Example - Brand/Flavour Cut to Growth Contribution Insight

Example Insight Translation
Raw cut finding: Lipton Peach Ice Tea (500ml PET) shows Contribution to Growth of 34% of total brand growth this YTD, while representing only 18% of Portfolio - a disproportionate growth driver relative to its current sales base.
"Lipton Peach 500ml PET is punching well above its portfolio weight on growth - recommend prioritising distribution expansion for this flavour/format ahead of the next quarter, particularly in TOTAL DOWNLINE where current distribution lags the category average."
